Catalog Information

"Bindman" refers by entry number to David Bindman, ed., William Blake: Catalogue of the Collection in the Fitzwilliam Museum Cambridge (Cambridge, England: W. Heffer and sons Ltd. For the Syndics of the Fitzwilliam Museum, 1970). Entry numbers 467 and higher appear in the Appendix to Bindman, pages 65-84, and correspond to the entry numbers in [Geoffrey Keynes], Bibliotheca Bibliographici: A Catalogue of the Library Formed by Geoffrey Keynes (London: The Trianon Press, 1964). Keynes bequeathed part of his Blake collection to the Fitzwilliam Museum in 1982. Some materials listed in the Bindman appendix representing the Keynes bequest have not (or at least not yet) been received by the Museum; other materials (e.g., The Marriage of Heaven and Hell copy E, no. 510 in Bibliotheca Bibliographici) that were received by the Museum are not listed in the Bindman appendix. Most of the books from the Keynes collection containing Blake's commercial book illustrations and listed in the Bindman appendix are in the Cambridge University Library. For a list of the Keynes bequest acquired by the Museum, see The Annual Reports of the Syndicate and of the Friends of the Fitzwilliam for the Year Ending 31 December 1985 (Cambridge: Fitzwilliam Museum, [1986]).
